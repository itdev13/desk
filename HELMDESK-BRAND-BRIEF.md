# HelmDesk — Visual Brief for Image Generation

Use this document as context when generating images (app icon, marketplace screenshots, marketing banners, social cards, etc.) for **HelmDesk**. It describes what the product is, who it's for, and the exact visual identity to stay on-brand.

---

## What HelmDesk is

**HelmDesk is a white-label helpdesk & ticketing app.** It turns inbound customer conversations (SMS, email, WhatsApp, live chat, social, etc.) into tracked support **tickets** with SLAs, a Kanban board, agent routing, and a reporting dashboard. Agencies install it, brand it as their own, and resell it to their clients.

**One-line pitch:** "Every customer message becomes a tracked ticket — with SLAs, routing, and a clean queue your team actually enjoys."

**The name/metaphor:** *Helm* = a ship's steering wheel. The brand leans on the idea of **steering / staying in control / being at the helm** of customer support. Calm command of a busy support operation.

### Core features (what screenshots/visuals should show)
- **Queue** — a list of tickets, each with a priority stripe, ref number (e.g. `HD-10`), subject, contact, channel, and a live **SLA countdown** (green → amber → red as a deadline nears).
- **Board** — a Kanban board with columns (New / Open / Pending / Resolved) and draggable ticket cards.
- **Dashboard** — KPI cards (Open, Overdue, Avg first reply, In-SLA %) plus a created-vs-resolved trend chart.
- **SLA tracking** — per-priority "first response" and "resolve" targets; breached tickets turn red.
- **Team** — agent roster with active/assignable toggles and seat usage.
- **Plans** — Starter / Team / Agency pricing tiers.

### Audience
Marketing agencies and their small-business clients. Professional but approachable — not enterprise-cold, not toy-playful. Think "a tool a busy support lead trusts."

## Visual identity

### Logo / icon
- A bold **"H" monogram** in a rounded-square tile.
- Tile background: **amber `#E0A24A`**; the "H" in dark ink or white.
- Style: clean, geometric, confident. A subtle nod to a **ship's helm/wheel** is welcome but must stay legible at 32px. Avoid literal clip-art wheels — keep it a modern SaaS mark.
- Works on both light and dark backgrounds. No gradients on the core mark; flat and crisp.

### Color palette (exact hex)
| Role | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Accent (primary)** | `#E0A24A` | Amber — the signature color. Buttons, highlights, the logo tile, active states. |
| Accent dark | `#B97E2C` | Amber text/hover, emphasis. |
| Accent soft | `#FBEEDC` | Pale amber wash — card backgrounds, callouts, highlights. |
| **Ink (near-black)** | `#0F1729` | Primary text, dark UI, the "slate console" feel. Deep navy-charcoal, NOT pure black. |
| Ink soft | `#2A3447` | Secondary dark surfaces. |
| Paper | `#F5F7FA` | App background — cool off-white. |
| Panel | `#FFFFFF` | Cards / surfaces. |
| Good (green) | `#2F9E6B` | In-SLA / success. |
| Warning | `#D9822B` | Approaching SLA. |
| Critical (red) | `#D64545` | Breached / overdue. |
| Info (blue) | `#3B6FB0` | Neutral status. |

**Palette in words:** warm **amber** as the hero accent against a cool **navy-charcoal ink** and soft off-white paper. Semantic green/amber/red only for ticket status — never as decorative accents.

### Typography
- Clean, modern **sans-serif** (Inter / SF / Geist family feel). Headings semi-bold to bold, tight tracking. Body comfortable and readable.
- A **monospace** face is used for small data bits (ticket refs, SLA timers, plan labels) — gives a subtle "console/ops" precision.

### UI aesthetic ("slate-console")
- Rounded corners (~9–14px radius), soft shadows, generous whitespace.
- Cards on a light paper background; the top nav is a row of pill-shaped tabs.
- Priority shown as a colored left **stripe** on ticket rows; status as small **pills/chips**.
- Feels calm, organized, and premium — the opposite of a cluttered inbox.

### Mood / tone
Confident, calm, in-control. "At the helm." Professional SaaS with warmth (the amber). Not corporate-sterile, not cutesy.

## Do / Don't for images

**Do**
- Lead with amber `#E0A24A` on ink `#0F1729` / off-white `#F5F7FA`.
- Show real support-ops context: tickets, SLA timers, a tidy queue, a Kanban board.
- Keep it clean, spacious, high-contrast, legible.
- Use the ship's-helm metaphor subtly if useful (steering, control, calm seas).

**Don't**
- ❌ Don't use the words **"GoHighLevel"**, **"HighLevel"**, or **"GHL"** anywhere in visuals — this is a white-label product.
- ❌ No purple-to-blue gradients, no generic "AI startup" look.
- ❌ No pure black (`#000`) — use the ink navy-charcoal instead.
- ❌ Don't make the helm/wheel literal or nautical-kitsch (no anchors, ropes, pirate vibes).
- ❌ Avoid clutter; whitespace is part of the brand.
